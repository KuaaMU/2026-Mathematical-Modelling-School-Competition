\subsection{Exploratory Data Analysis}

\subsubsection{Temporal Trends Analysis}

Historical water consumption patterns reveal distinct phases:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Growth Phase (2000-2007)}: Rapid increase averaging 2.8\% annually
    \item \textbf{Transition Phase (2008-2012)}: Volatile growth with policy interventions
    \item \textbf{Stabilization Phase (2013-2016)}: Plateau around 600 billion m$^3$
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Correlation Analysis}

Key relationships identified through correlation analysis:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{GDP-Water Correlation}: Strong positive correlation (r=0.89) with diminishing intensity over time
    \item \textbf{Population-Residential Water}: High correlation (r=0.94) indicating demographic driver
    \item \textbf{Industrial Structure}: Negative correlation between heavy industry share and water efficiency
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Regional Variations (Beijing Case)}

Beijing municipal data reveals urban water consumption characteristics:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Higher Price Sensitivity}: Urban consumers show greater responsiveness to price changes
    \item \textbf{Income Effects}: Strong correlation between per capita income and residential water use
    \item \textbf{Policy Impact}: Clear response to water conservation campaigns and pricing reforms
\end{itemize}
